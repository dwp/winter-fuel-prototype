How much you'll get

If you’re eligible for a Winter Fuel Payment, you’ll get a letter telling you how much you’ll get each year.

The amount you get is based on when you were born and your circumstances between 19 to 25 September 2022. This is called the ‘qualifying week’.

Your payment is affected by:

  • who you live with
  • if you live in a care home
  • if you get certain benefits

Tell the Winter Fuel Payment Centre if your circumstances have changed.

The amounts listed include a 'Pensioner Cost of Living Payment'. This is between £150 and £300. You’ll only get this extra amount in winter 2022 to 2023.

Any money you get is tax-free and will not affect your other benefits.

If you live alone or no one you live with is eligible for the Winter Fuel Payment

You’ll get either:

  • £500 if you were born between 26 September 1942 and 25 September 1956
  • £600 if you were born before 26 September 1942

If you live with someone who's eligible for the Winter Fuel Payment

Your payment may be different if you or the person you live with gets one of the following benefits:

  • Pension Credit
  • Income Support
  • income-based Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)
  • income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)

If you do not get any of the benefits

You’ll get a payment of either:

  • £250 if you were born between 26 September 1942 and 25 September 1956
  • £350 if you were born before 26 September 1942 but the person you live with was born after 25 September 1956
  • £300 if you and the person you live with were both born before 26 September 1942

You and the person you live with will get separate payments - they might get more if they get one of the benefits.

If you and your partner jointly claim any of the benefits

One of you will get a payment of either:

  • £500 if both of you were born between 26 September 1942 and 25 September 1956
  • £600 if one or both of you were born before 26 September 1942
The payment will go to the person who claims benefits or the ‘main claimant’. For example, the person who first applied for Pension Credit.

If you get any of the benefits (not as part of a joint claim)

You’ll get a payment of either:

  • £500 if you were born between 26 September 1942 and 25 September 1956
  • £600 if you were born before 26 September 1942

You and the person you live with will get separate payments - they might get less if they do not get one of the benefits.

If you live in a care home

If you live in a care home and you're eligible you'll get either:

  • £250 if you were born between 26 September 1942 and 25 September 1956
  • £300 if you were born before 26 September 1942
